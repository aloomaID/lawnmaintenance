Plantation Florida experiences a subtropical climate, characterized by hot, humid summers and mild winters. This climate supports a variety of grass types, including Bermuda, Zoysia, and St. Augustine, which thrive in warm temperatures and can withstand the occasional drought.
2. Soil Health:
The quality of your lawn starts with the soil. Conducting a soil test can provide insights into pH levels and nutrient deficiencies. In Plantation Florida sandy soils are common, which may require regular amendments with organic matter to improve water retention and nutrient availability.
3. Watering Practices:
Effective irrigation is vital in Plantation Florida due to the region's warm climate. Lawns typically require 1 to 1.5 inches of water per week. Early morning watering is recommended to minimize evaporation and fungal diseases.

Common pests in Plantation Florida such as grubs and chinch bugs, can damage your lawn. Integrated Pest Management (IPM) strategies, including regular monitoring and the use of eco-friendly treatments, can effectively control these pests while protecting beneficial insects.

Plantation, FL Insights: Population, Influence, and Service Areas

Plantation is a city in Broward County, Florida, United States. It is a part of the South Florida metropolitan area. The city's name comes from the previous part-owner of the land, the Everglades Plantation Company, and their unsuccessful attempts to establish a rice plantation in the area. As of the 2020 U.S. census, the population was 91,750.
