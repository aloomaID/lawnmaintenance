2. Soil Health:
The quality of your lawn starts with the soil. Conducting a soil test can provide insights into pH levels and nutrient deficiencies. In Freeburg Illinois sandy soils are common, which may require regular amendments with organic matter to improve water retention and nutrient availability.

Effective irrigation is vital in Freeburg Illinois due to the region's warm climate. Lawns typically require 1 to 1.5 inches of water per week. Early morning watering is recommended to minimize evaporation and fungal diseases.

Common pests in Freeburg Illinois such as grubs and chinch bugs, can damage your lawn. Integrated Pest Management (IPM) strategies, including regular monitoring and the use of eco-friendly treatments, can effectively control these pests while protecting beneficial insects.
