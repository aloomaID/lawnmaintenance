Farmington Hills Michigan experiences a subtropical climate, characterized by hot, humid summers and mild winters. This climate supports a variety of grass types, including Bermuda, Zoysia, and St. Augustine, which thrive in warm temperatures and can withstand the occasional drought.
2. Soil Health:
The quality of your lawn starts with the soil. Conducting a soil test can provide insights into pH levels and nutrient deficiencies. In Farmington Hills Michigan sandy soils are common, which may require regular amendments with organic matter to improve water retention and nutrient availability.
3. Watering Practices:
Effective irrigation is vital in Farmington Hills Michigan due to the region's warm climate. Lawns typically require 1 to 1.5 inches of water per week. Early morning watering is recommended to minimize evaporation and fungal diseases.
